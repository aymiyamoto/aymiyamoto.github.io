Maintenance mechanisms of the wintertime subtropical high over the south Indian Ocean

Climatologically the surface Mascarene high over the subtropical south Indian Ocean shifts westward toward austral winter, and its strength as a planetary-wave component maximizes in late austral winter, unlike its counterpart over other subtropical oceans. While the maintenance mechanisms of the summertime Mascarene high was investigated by previous studies such as Miyamoto et al. (2021), dynamics of the wintertime high has not been fully understood yet. The present study proposes that both the Asian summer monsoon and the enhanced storm-track activity maintained by the Agulhas SST front externally modulate the positively coupled system between the wintertime Mascarene high and low-level clouds to realize its unique seasonality.
  • Miyamoto, A., H. Nakamura, T. Miyasaka, Y. Kosaka, B. Taguchi, and K. Nishii, 2022, Maintenance mechanisms of the wintertime subtropical high over the south Indian Ocean, Journal of Climate, 35, 2989-3005, https://doi.org/10.1175/JCLI-D-21-0518.1.
